Quantitative Fundamentals: Company

Dimension Overview

This section focuses on the quantifiable aspects of a company's financial performance and position. Quantitative fundamentals provide a numerical basis for evaluating a stock's potential value and growth prospects.

Revenue Growth

Year-over-year revenue growth measures how quickly a company is expanding.

Profit Margins

Margins indicate how efficiently a company converts revenue into profits.

Return on Equity

ROE measures how effectively a company uses shareholder investments.

Key Indicators

When evaluating stocks through this dimension, focus on consistent trends rather than single-period metrics. Long-term stability in these numbers often indicates a well-managed company with sustainable advantages.
